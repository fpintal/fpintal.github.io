Website Evaluations

Below I will show two websites, they will be evaluated by the CRAP principles

Apple

Apple’s site highlights products with clean layouts, large visuals, and simple text. It’s easy to navigate and reflects the brand’s polished, minimalist style.

  • Contrast: Apple uses strong contrast—white space against bold product images, dark vs. light backgrounds—to make content easy to read and highlight the product focus.
  • Repitition: Consistent use of fonts, navigation bar placement, and clean product grids creates a predictable, professional flow.
  • Alignment: Everything follows a strict grid; text, images, and buttons are perfectly aligned, which makes the site look clean and polished.
  • Proximity: Related items (like product details, purchase links, and accessories) are grouped together, so users can quickly connect related information.

Yale School of Art

The Yale School of Art site is creative but chaotic. Clashing colors, scattered text, and inconsistent layouts make information hard to find.

  • Contrast: Overuse of clashing neon colors, animations, and flashing elements makes it hard to focus on what matters.
  • Repitition: Instead of consistent styles, every section looks different—buttons, fonts, and layouts change unpredictably, which confuses users.
  • Alignment: Content is scattered with little respect for a grid; elements feel randomly placed, leading to a chaotic experience.
  • Proximity: Too much unrelated information is crammed together (ads, offers, text blocks, GIFs), making it difficult to find the actual leasing options.
